Background About Zion National Park

Some background about Zion National Park is that it is a canyon just like the Grand Canyon however the river that formed it is called the Virgin River. It was founded in 1919 the same year as the Grand Canyon. Zion Canyon is a mix of erosion and river breaking through rock and has been in formation for nearly 240 million years. There are several trails in Zion but some of the more popular trails are the Narrows and Angels Landing trails. Angels Landing is a long zig-zag up a mountain until you get to the top where you can see the entire park with an astounding view. The Narrows is a 16-mile hike through a river with massive canyon walls on either side.

My Experience At Zion National Park

The last time I was able to visit Zion I was able to hike Angels Landing. Angels Landing Is a pretty tough hike being 5.4 miles and having an elevation gain of nearly 1500 feet. Last summer I was able to hike the Narrows and Angels Landing was nothing compared to the Narrows. It's pretty much a 16-mile hike through a river however the water is only around 2 feet high for most of the hike. There are some spots that you might have to swim at but it's not too bad. The Narrows were not too hard for me but some of my family members got lost and had to spend the night in the canyon.
