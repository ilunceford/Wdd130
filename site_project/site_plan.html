My Expirience At Grand Canyon National Park

To begin I chose to put Grand Canyon first because it is my favorite national park of the three I will be talking about. As a little background about the Grand Canyon, It was officially founded in 1919 but has been in formation for about 5 million years. The canyon was formed by the Colorado River which carved its way through rock still even today. The width of the Grand Canyon is massive with some parts being as wide as 18 miles. The full-length of the Grand Canyon is about 227 miles in length and is the second most visited national park in all of the United States. The last time I visited I was with my family and we were able to hike around 8 miles of the South Rim Trail. We started our trip by driving for four and a half hours until we arrived and got in line to board a shuttle. I mentioned earlier that a lot of people visit it and the lines are very long. Once we got on the shuttle we drove to the trailhead where we hiked along the southern rim trail. The trail was beautiful the whole way because you had a glorious view of the Grand Canyon the entire way. That is also why it is my favorite park because it has the best view and will knock your socks off.

My Expirience At Zion National Park

Some background about Zion National Park is that it is a canyon just like the Grand Canyon however the river that formed it is called the Virgin River. It was founded in 1919 the same year as the Grand Canyon. Zion Canyon is a mix of erosion and river breaking through rock and has been in formation for nearly 240 million years. There are several trails in Zion but some of the more popular trails are the Narrows and Angels Landing trails. Angels Landing is a long zig-zag up a mountain until you get to the top where you can see the entire park with an astounding view. The Narrows is a 16-mile hike through a river with massive canyon walls on either side. The last time I was able to visit Zion I was able to hike Angels Landing. Angels Landing Is a pretty tough hike being 5.4 miles and having an elevation gain of nearly 1500 feet. Last summer I was able to hike the Narrows and Angels Landing was nothing compared to the Narrows. It's pretty much a 16-mile hike through a river however the water is only around 2 feet high for most of the hike. There are some spots that you might have to swim at but it's not too bad. The Narrows were not too hard for me but some of my family members got lost and had to spend the night in the canyon.

My Expirience At Arches National Park And Q&A

A little background about Arches is it has around 2000 natural sandstone arches and was founded in 1929. The arches have been in formation for nearly 15 million years and were formed when erosion impacted sedimentary rock and formed arches. Arches and Zion are both in Utah but Arches is more famous because on the Utah license plate, you can find one of the famous arches pictured. The last time I was able to visit was this previous November and we were able to hike many trails and view many different arches. My favorite arch is called the Double Arch because it is the only major double arch in the entire park. You can climb up as well and I was able to capture some pretty good photography of the double arch. My favorite trail however is the broken arch trail because it has some of the best views of the different arches. It's a round trip of only 1.2 miles but has several different arches.
